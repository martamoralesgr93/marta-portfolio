# Design & UX Strategy

### 1. Problem Framing
The tool had grown functionally, but not from a unified experience logic. Many modules responded to specific business or academic management needs, but the overall experience lacked consistency and clarity for the end user. There was a disconnect between the technical structure and the user's actual understanding, forcing them to interpret too many visual decisions before completing simple tasks.

### 2. UX Strategy & Information Architecture
Based on the analysis, I worked on reorganising the experience by prioritising cognitive clarity, visual hierarchy, consistency and ease of use. The strategy focused on simplifying navigation, reorganising modules by usage priority, improving scannability and reducing unnecessary visual load. I also redefined content structures and component interaction patterns.

### 3. Accessibility & Inclusive Design (WCAG)
Accessibility was a key part of the project. I worked on improvements related to contrast, semantic hierarchy, keyboard navigation, interactive states and visual consistency. The goal wasn't just to meet WCAG criteria, but to build a more usable and comprehensible experience for all user profiles. Many visual and structural decisions were made from an accessibility-first and cognitive-load-reduction perspective.

### 4. Design System Thinking
I established a systematic logic within the interface to reduce module inconsistencies, defining reusable patterns, common hierarchies, consistent behaviours and shared visual rules (design tokens) to ensure system scalability.
